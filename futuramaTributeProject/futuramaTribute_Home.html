Charles Babbage Tribute Page

Charles Babbage is a British polymath, skilled in mathematics, philosophy, mechanical engineering, and was an inventor. Babbage played an integral role in creating the Royal Astronomers society, which aimed at promoting the use of math for practical applications, such as navigation. While Babbage invented the cow catcher for locomotives, his most acclaimed creations were the difference engine and the blueprints for a more complex machine: the Analytical engine. The difference engine was the world's first digital computer, which was able to break complex math calculations into simpler subtraction and addition problems by representing numbers 0-9 as teeth on gears. This device was able to perform math calculations more accurately and efficiently than human computers at the time.
